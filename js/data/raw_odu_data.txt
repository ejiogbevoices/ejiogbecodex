1. Èjì Ogbè
Èjì Ogbè.
Ifá, Orí (personal destiny/consciousness), Òrìṣànlá (Ọbàtálá), Èṣù Ọdàrà, Ọ̀ṣun, Ẹgbẹ́.
Life, Beginning, Light, Clarity, Creation. Represents the Elder who opens the world through prayer and the dawn of life, illumination, and divine potential. The child born under this Odù has the Orí of accomplishment and fortune.
Must never aim beyond being the number Three in life to avoid trouble and restlessness. Must practice patience and perseverance. Must not allow losses to cause worry, as there is hope as long as there is life.
2. Òyèkú Méjì
Òyèkú Méjì.
Ifá, Orí, Olódùmarè.
Endings, Transformation, Darkness. Represents nightfall and the womb that gestates light. Explains that death is a natural phenomenon and not destruction.
Must maintain hope and perseverance until death.
3. Ìwòrì Méjì
Ìwòrì Méjì, Eleji-Iwori.
Òrúnmìlà, Ògún, Odù, Òrìṣà Òṣèèrèmògbò (Ọbàtálá).
Transformation, Consciousness, Blueprint of self and psyche. Defines nature, direction, and acceptance. Emphasizes the need for cooperation to accomplish tasks.
Must offer ẹbọ. Must show gratitude to Olódùmarè. Warns against wickedness towards others, even if they show ingratitude.
4. Òdí Méjì
Òdí Méjì, Fine dust (alias).
Ifá, Odù (the source of Babaláwo power), Òrúnmìlà, Òrìṣànlá (Òòṣà), Àjé (witches).
Temporary transformation and the establishment of a foundation. Deals with the mystery of rebirth and repeating cycles.
Must not pluck the fruit of Juwa (i.e., not seize unearned benefits). If facing setback, must offer ẹbọ with liquor to serve Ifá and Òòṣànlá. Must desist from a dangerous game.
5. Ìrosùn Méjì
Ìrosùn Méjì.
Ifá, Òrìṣà Oko, Egúngún (Ancestors), Ọ̀ṣun.
Ancestry, Destiny, Bloodlines. Focuses on dignity or disgrace rooted in lineage. Linked to patience and perseverance to move from nothing to abundance.
If pursuing progress, must persevere because something is always chasing or being pursued.
6. Òwónrín Méjì
Òwónrín Méjì.
Ifá, Èṣù Ọdàrà, Oro.
Change, Movement, Instability (implied in verses, but no formal thematic definition in these sources).
Must exercise patience and perseverance; success comes only on the third attempt (third marriage or business venture). Warns of instability caused by restlessness or lack of focus.
7. Òbàrà Méjì
Òbàrà Méjì.
Ifá, Ajé (wealth), Ṣàngó.
Prosperity, Wealth, Inner Transformation, Strength. Associated with words, communication, and social order. Teaches that empathy and humility are necessary for success.
Warns against the habit of denying all the good things Ifá had done. Must imbibe the value of showing appreciation. Cautions against being inexplicit in speech, which may cause trouble.
8. Òkànràn Méjì
Òkànràn Méjì, Olokanran.
Ifá, Ṣàngó, Ògún, Èṣù Ọdàrà.
Conflict, Struggle, Testing of Character (implied). Associated with the transformative value of discipline and endurance.
Must not think wrongly, speak wrongly, or do wrongly. Must not think he/she has no rival; this attitude is misconduct. Must comply with warnings/taboos to avoid negative consequences from Èṣù Ọdàrà.
9. Ògúndá Méjì
Ògúndá Méjì.
Orí, Ògún, Ifá, Ọbàtálá, Olódùmarè, Ayan.
Accomplishment, Fortune, Labor, Iron. If in Ire, signifies the Orí of accomplishment and fortune. Linked to truthfulness and vindication.
Need for patience and perseverance. Must not wrong anyone or be wicked. Warns that one might possess blessings that never reach fruition (Àmúbọ̀).
10. Òsá Méjì
Òsá Méjì.
Ọ̀yá (Iansã), Iyàmí Òṣòrongà (Witches/Mothers), Odù, Ògún, Ọbàtálá, Olókun, Egúngún, Èṣù Ọdàrà.
Secrets, Hidden Forces, Transformation, Witches/Aje. Associated with sudden, radical, purifying change. Focuses on the power of the Mothers/feminine principle.
Must be handled with caution due to association with witchcraft. Must seek truth and use patience. Males must prostrate themselves before Odù (the womb) and woman. Must never use a lie in the name of destiny.
11. Ìká Méjì
Ìká Méjì.
Ifá, Èṣù Ọdàrà, Ògún, Ọbàtálá, Ancestors.
Struggle, Discipline, Karmic Web. The Odù of confrontation through consequence. Linked to sacred revelation and cleansing.
Whoever plants the yam of wickedness, the evil will grow on his own head. Must never wish anyone evil. Cautions against arrogance, recklessness, and misuse of power.
12. Òtúrúpòn Méjì
Òtúrúpòn Méjì.
Ifá, Òòṣà (Ọbàrìṣà/Ọbàtálá), Èṣù, Òrìsà Oko, Ṣàngó.
Abundance and Renewal. Focuses on the value of persistence over mere performance.
Must ask carefully and often, and not fake knowing. Must honor the earth (Orisha Oko); ignoring him causes crops to fail and ailments.
13. Òtúrá Méjì
Òtúrá Méjì.
Ọ̀rúnmìlà, Ọbàtálá, Ṣàngó, Ọ̀yá, Ọ̀ṣun, Yemọ̀, Èṣù Ọdàrà, Ẹ̀gbé, Òrò.
Wisdom, Guidance, Orientation. Deals with supernatural strength from human speech. The Odù of ascension and ruin.
Must exercise clearness of thought, discernment, patience, and humility. Must be consulted for guidance. Must not consume alcohol in excess or wear black or red dresses (wrath of Ọbàtálá).
14. Ìretè Méjì
Ìretè Méjì.
Ifá, Olofin, Orí, Ṣàngó.
Prosperity, Abundance, Sacred Rebellion. The Odù of healing the truth beneath the truth.
Warns that failing to offer ẹbọ can lead to a "sack of ailments" causing death. Must not lie, and must feed Orí before pride.
15. Òsé Méjì
Òsé Méjì, Ejiosẹ.
Ifá, Ọ̀ṣun, Ògún, Ṣàngó, Èṣù, Egún, Yéwá.
Sweetness, Fertility, Sustaining Feminine Energy. The engine of all transformation. Restores harmony.
Compliance to ẹbọ is supportive; non-compliance is not. Must never argue. Must ensure there is no misconduct or thinking one has no rival.
16. Òfún Méjì
Òfún Méjì, Òràngún-Méjì, Yéèpà Odù.
Ifá, Òrìṣànlá (Òrìṣàlá), Odù, Elégbára (Èṣù).
Purity, Completion, Authority. Represents the Ocean of Completion (implied) and reveals truth in its fullness. Formerly primary, downgraded due to arrogance.
Must not do things against the norms of society. Must never betray loved ones. Must maintain moral integrity and avoid arrogance. Must never be intolerant.  Odù Name (Alias)
